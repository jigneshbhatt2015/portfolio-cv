Revolutionizing eCommerce Search with Generative AI


The AI-powered site-search experience has proved to be a game-changer by providing a far more engaging and personalized experience, listing the products in search results based on their purchasing history, browsing patterns, and other chats it must be having with chatbots or assistants. 44% of online shoppers start their purchasing journey through the search bar. Google research exemplifies this point by stating that 53% of U.S. online consumers abandon their carts and go elsewhere if they can’t find what they’re looking for on a site.

Advantages: AI-based search populates its search results by intuitively matching the terms or phrases entered by users to better understand the user's context and intent, not by matching the exact keyword types in the search bar. This gives quick and accurate results to shoppers, which increases their likelihood of completing the sale on their shopping website. Using machine learning algorithms, AI-based search builds a deeper understanding of the user’s search query, its’ browsing behavior, the retailer's product catalog, and the data on popular results. Using large language models (LLMs), it generates responses accurate enough to match what shoppers are looking for, thereby increasing the likelihood of improving conversion rates.

Challenges: While AI-based search algorithms are advantageous, there’re some downsides as well that businesses need to be wary of. The accuracy with which the algorithm predicts largely depends on the quality of the data it has been trained on. There are chances it can give irrelevant search results based on the interpretation it makes of the search keywords and product attributes. Also, Gen AI algorithms may unintentionally reinforce prejudice in product suggestions. For instance, an AI system used by a cosmetic business might neglect to consider the demands of customers with darker complexions if it habitually suggests lighter makeup shades to those with lighter skin tones. Knowing these weaknesses, retailers have started adopting the hybrid approach—a blend of traditional keyword matching with these smart AI-search features, taking advantage of AI’s automation efficiencies while maintaining the keyword-based search strategy to avoid the blunders that AI algorithms can potentially produce. Building accuracy with AI models also requires a bit of human intervention, as they would manually tinker with their search keywords to populate the right results, which in turn benefits the AI-search algorithm, which is flexible enough to apply reinforcement learning to continually optimize shoppers’ search results.

Implementation: Google’s Gemini 1.5 Pro is the largest LLM on the market so far in terms of its “context window." It has the capability to process around 700,000 words of context, making it powerful in generating more accurate and contextually richer responses. It is based on the RAG (retrieval augmented generation) framework, which combines information retrieval with text generation and enriches the LLM knowledge base with your business context. AI search typically involves three steps in terms of query processing, retrieval, and ranking.

Google Cloud announced its flagship search technology, which uses LLM capabilities to enable shoppers to find its products easily and conveniently based on their search patterns. Its model is Vertex AI search, which is adopted by many retailers. Of course, Google is not the only provider of AI-search solutions; there are many companies clouded in this commerce search market, namely Algolia, Coveo, and Microsoft Azure’s OpenAI service.

AI-based search is one of the ways to bring in personalization for users, which is paving the way in digital e-commerce. It depends on the strength of the data and your algorithm’s ability to process and act on the data. AI-based search coupled with conversational AI can be viewed as a complete personalized experience that shoppers get while searching for their products, and hence, Gen AI seems to be a preferred alternative for shoppers who are bored of traversing faceted search, review platforms, third-party publishers, blogs, and so on.
